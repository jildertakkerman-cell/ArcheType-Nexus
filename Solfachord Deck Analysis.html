Solfachord

A Conductor's Guide to the Pendulum Symphony

An Archetypal Overture

The Solfachord archetype presents a unique and intricate approach to Pendulum Summoning. Thematically based on the musical scale, these Fairy-type monsters are designed around balancing odd and even Pendulum Scales to unlock a variety of protective and offensive effects. Recent support has transformed the archetype from a novel concept into a highly consistent and explosive combo engine, allowing the entire Solfachord orchestra to perform at a competitive level.

Meet the Musicians

Starters & Searchers

The premier one-card starter. Searches any "Solfachord" card and provides negation protection for your activated "Solfachord" card effects.

SolSolfachord Gracia

Extenders & Bosses

Solfachord Solfeggia

A free Special Summon that extends your plays and helps establish the widest possible Pendulum scales (0 to 9).

ReSolfachord Dreamia

A simple but vital extender that Special Summons itself from the hand, providing material for Link Summons.

GranSolfachord Coolia

The ultimate in-archetype boss monster. A Link-3 that provides a powerful, repeatable omni-negate.

Facing the Critic

Strengths

  • Unprecedented Consistency: A high density of one-card starters makes the deck incredibly reliable.
  • High Ceiling: Capable of producing multi-negate boards that compete with top-tier strategies.
  • Built-in Resilience: Can play through some disruption thanks to multiple starters and negation protection.

Weaknesses

  • Normal Summon Reliant: The entire combo is often bottlenecked through a single Normal Summon.
  • Extra Deck Vulnerability: Highly susceptible to floodgates that restrict Special Summoning.
  • Known Choke Points: Negating the starter or Heavymetalfoes Electrumite is often a game-ending play.

External Engines & Tech

  • Pendulum Engine: Uses Heavymetalfoes Electrumite and Astrograph Sorcerer for explosive advantage.
  • Fairy Engine: Diviner of the Herald and Trias Hierarchia provide an alternative, non-Pendulum starter.
  • Magician Package: Cards like Harmonizing Magician enable easy access to Synchro monsters like Borreload Savage Dragon.

TCG Banlist Impact

The Solfachord archetype is in a unique position. While the core archetype is completely unrestricted, its competitive ceiling is heavily influenced by the banlist's treatment of generic Pendulum and Extra Deck support.

Key Generic Hits

  • Heavymetalfoes Electrumite (Forbidden): The loss of the premier generic Pendulum Link monster significantly hampers combo potential.
  • Apollousa, Bow of the Goddess (Forbidden): A common and powerful end board piece for monster negation is unavailable.
  • Borreload Savage Dragon (Forbidden): The go-to Synchro for an omni-negate is no longer an option for the deck's end board.

Unrestricted Core

  • All "Solfachord" main deck monsters (3 copies each)
  • All "Solfachord" Extra Deck monsters
  • All "Solfachord" Spell/Trap support cards
  • Full access to the Diviner of the Herald engine

Meta Implications: Solfachord's internal consistency remains at its peak, allowing it to start its plays reliably. However, the prohibition of its most powerful generic end board pieces forces the deck to rely more on its in-archetype boss monster, GranSolfachord Coolia, and other alternatives like I:P Masquerena plays. While still potent, the deck's overall power ceiling is lower than it would be with access to these forbidden staples.
