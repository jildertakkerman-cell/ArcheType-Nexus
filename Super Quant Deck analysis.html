SUPER QUANT

Pilots, Zords, and the Path to Great Magnus

Archetype Doctrine

Inspired by the Super Sentai (Power Rangers) genre, the Super Quant archetype is a go-first combo strategy with a singular objective: assemble the ultimate Megazord. Individual "Super Quantum" pilots command their color-coordinated "Super Quantal Mech Beast" Xyz monsters. These machines then combine to form the colossal boss monster, Super Quantal Mech King Great Magnus. The entire deck is engineered to facilitate this final assembly, turning every duel into a mission to build the team and unleash its combined power.

The Ranger Corps & Command Center

The Pilots: Core Monsters

Super Quantum Blue Layer

The most important card. On summon, it searches any "Super Quant" card, providing unparalleled consistency.

Super Quantum Red Layer

A primary extender and recovery agent. It can Special Summon itself and retrieve resources from the Graveyard.

Super Quantum Green Layer

A supplementary extender that Special Summons another "Super Quant" monster from hand.

Super Quantal Fairy Alphan

The deck's most explosive combo starter, capable of setting up the entire board from a single activation.

The Command Center: Key Spells

Super Quantal Mech Ship Magnacarrier

The Field Spell that enables the entire strategy, allowing pilots to summon their Mech Beasts and combine into Great Magnus.

Emergency Teleport

A crucial consistency tool that Special Summons Blue Layer directly from the deck to start your combos.

It's Morphin' Time!: The "Telephan" Combo

The deck's premier opening play requires Emergency Teleport and Super Quantal Fairy Alphan. This combination demonstrates how the Super Quant cards synergize to build a dominant board from just two cards.

The Alphan Play

Normal Summon Alphan and Tribute it. This summons one "Super Quantum" monster and sends two others to the GY, triggering multiple effects to swarm the field.

Assemble the Megazord

Use Magnacarrier repeatedly to summon your Mech Beasts, then use its final effect to combine them into Great Magnus with 6+ materials.

The Final Formation: The Great Magnus Endboard

Super Quantal Mech King Great Magnus

Super Quantal Mech King Great Magnus

The ultimate boss monster. Its power scales with the number of materials it has:

  • 2+ Materials: Quick Effect to shuffle any card on the field into the deck (non-targeting removal).
  • 4+ Materials: Becomes unaffected by all other card effects, making it a nearly indestructible tower.
  • 6+ Materials: Your opponent cannot add cards from their Deck to their hand, a devastating floodgate that shuts down most modern strategies.

While incredibly powerful, its main vulnerability is non-effect removal, such as being Tributed by a Kaiju monster.

Competitive Analysis

Strengths

  • High Ceiling: Capable of producing one of the most oppressive boss monsters in the game.
  • Consistent Combos: Multiple two-card combinations lead to the same powerful endboard.
  • Rogue Threat: Unfamiliarity with the deck's choke points can lead to opponent misplays.

Weaknesses

  • Fragile Choke Points: A single hand trap on Alphan or Blue Layer can end the turn.
  • Vulnerable to Kaijus: Great Magnus has a well-known weakness to being Tributed.
  • Glass Cannon: The deck is all-in on its main combo and can struggle to recover if disrupted.

Current TCG Banlist Status

MINIMAL IMPACT: The Super Quant archetype remains almost entirely at full power, with only one generic support card on the list.

Limited Cards (1 Copy)

  • Emergency Teleport - Key consistency tool for summoning Blue Layer

Unrestricted Cards

  • All "Super Quantum" Main Deck monsters
  • All "Super Quantal Mech Beast" Xyz monsters
  • Super Quantal Mech Ship Magnacarrier
  • Super Quantal Mech King Great Magnus

Meta Implications: With its core engine completely untouched, Super Quant operates at near-peak efficiency. The limitation of "Emergency Teleport" slightly reduces the deck's consistency in accessing its primary starter, but the deck has enough alternative lines of play to remain a potent and consistent combo strategy.
