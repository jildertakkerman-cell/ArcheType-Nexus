Sacred Beasts

Unleashing the Phantasms

Executive Summary

The Sacred Beasts—Uria, Hamon, and Raviel—began as anime icons with immense power but impractical summoning conditions. Modern support has transformed them, shifting focus from their original summoning methods to a consistent engine that "cheats" them onto the field. This report analyzes how the Sacred Beasts evolved from three unwieldy titans into the synergistic core of a potent control and One-Turn Kill (OTK) deck, capable of establishing oppressive board states and ending duels with overwhelming force. The strategy now revolves around a suite of support cards designed to deploy the Three Phantoms with remarkable efficiency.

The Engine Room

The Prime Movers

The deck's consistency hinges on a core of 0 ATK/DEF Fiend monsters that search and summon the Sacred Beasts.

  • Dark Beckoning Beast: The one-card starter. On Normal Summon, it searches any "Sacred Beast" card and grants an additional Normal Summon for another 0 ATK/DEF Fiend.
  • Chaos Summoning Beast: Tributes itself to summon a Sacred Beast from the hand. Its GY effect banishes itself to search "Fallen Paradise".
  • Dark Summoning Beast: Tributes itself to summon a Sacred Beast from the hand or Deck, but prevents you from attacking that turn.

The Central Hub

The deck's spells provide recursion, protection, and card advantage, forming a resilient loop.

  • Opening of the Spirit Gates: A Continuous Spell that searches a monster on activation, revives a 0 ATK/DEF Fiend from the GY by discarding, and recycles a Continuous Spell from the GY.
  • Fallen Paradise: The Field Spell. While you control a Sacred Beast, it provides targeting and destruction protection and lets you draw 2 cards once per turn.

The Titans: Role and Application

Raviel, Lord of Phantasms

The primary beater. A 4000 ATK/DEF wall that enables a devastating OTK with its support card, Shimmering Scraper, which doubles its ATK to 8000 and allows it to attack all monsters.

Hamon, Lord of Striking Thunder

The defensive juggernaut. While in Defense Position, it forces the opponent to attack it. Its support spell, Cerulean Skyfire, provides a Spell/Trap negate, then swaps Hamon to Defense to activate its protection.

Uria, Lord of Searing Flames

The backrow punisher. It gains 1000 ATK for each Continuous Trap in the GY and can destroy a Set Spell/Trap once per turn. Its trap, Hyper Blaze, fuels its ATK and provides revival for any Sacred Beast.

The Grand Finale: Typical End Boards

The deck's goal is to establish a protected boss monster or, with a stronger hand, a complete lockdown of the opponent's monster effects.

The Core Fortress

  • A Sacred Beast (e.g., Raviel): A 4000 ATK/DEF body that's difficult to remove by battle.
  • Fallen Paradise: Provides complete targeting and destruction protection for your Beasts.
  • Card Advantage: The "Draw 2" from Fallen Paradise refills your hand with disruption or follow-up plays.

The "High Roll" Lockdown

  • Two+ Sacred Beasts: Achieved with Opening of the Spirit Gates, getting two different Beasts on field.
  • Awakening of the Sacred Beasts: With two Beasts, this Continuous Trap gains its devastating floodgate effect.
  • The Lock: "Negate the activated effects of monsters your opponent controls." This is a one-sided Skill Drain that paralyzes most decks.

Current TCG Banlist Status

MODERATE IMPACT: While the core Sacred Beast monsters are unrestricted, key consistency and synergy cards are limited, forcing the deck to adapt.

Limited Cards (1 per Deck)

  • Opening of the Spirit Gates - A major hit to consistency and recovery.
  • Bystial Magnamhut - Key synergy piece for Branded variants is limited.

Semi-Limited Cards (2 per Deck)

  • Branded Fusion - Slightly impacts the consistency of Branded-hybrid builds.

Unrestricted Cards (3 per Deck)

  • All Sacred Beasts (Uria, Hamon, Raviel) & their support (Shimmering Scraper, etc.)
  • Dark Beckoning Beast, Chaos Summoning Beast, Dark Summoning Beast
  • Fallen Paradise, Awakening of the Sacred Beasts, Dimension Fusion Destruction

Meta Implications: The limit on Opening of the Spirit Gates significantly hurts the deck's resilience and ability to "high roll" into the two-beast lockdown. The deck must rely more heavily on Dark Beckoning Beast or external engines to function, making it more vulnerable to a single interruption.

Competitive Analysis

Strengths

  • High Consistency: Powerful one-card starters like Dark Beckoning Beast.
  • Strong Resilience: Fallen Paradise provides excellent protection, and the engine has many recovery loops.
  • Powerful End Boards: Can end on a 10,000 ATK OTK machine, a protected boss, or a one-sided Skill Drain.

Weaknesses

  • Normal Summon Reliant: The pure build is extremely vulnerable to hand traps (Ash, Imperm) on Dark Beckoning Beast.
  • Floodgate Vulnerability: Susceptible to cards like Macro Cosmos or Summon Limit that stop its engine.
  • Linear Combos: The main combo lines are predictable, allowing experienced opponents to disrupt key chokepoints.

External Engines & Hybrids

  • Branded & Bystial: The most competitive variant. Branded Fusion acts as an alternate starter, setting up the GY for Spirit Gates.
  • Eldlich: Synergizes with the focus on Continuous Traps, fueling Uria and recycling "Golden Land" cards with Awakening.
  • Dogmatika: Nadir Servant can send monsters to the GY while Ecclesia provides on-field negation.
