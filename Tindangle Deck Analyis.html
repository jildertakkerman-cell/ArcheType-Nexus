Tindangle

The Geometry of Terror

Executive Summary

The Tindangle archetype is a unique collection of DARK Fiend monsters defined by their FLIP effects and deep reliance on the Graveyard as a resource engine. Inspired by the eldritch "Hounds of Tindalos," their strategy is a patient grind game. The goal is to survive the early game by loading the Graveyardâ€”a metaphorical "angle of time"â€”with key monsters. From this safe dimension, they emerge en masse, typically on Turn 3, to unleash a decisive counter-attack and secure victory.

The Tindangle Ensemble

Core Monsters & Enablers

Tindangle Jhrelth

The premier one-card starter. Discards a card to send any "Tindangle" card from Deck to GY and summons itself face-down.

Tindangle Intruder

A key searcher and GY enabler. Its FLIP effect searches any "Tindangle" card, and it revives itself from the GY.

Tindangle Dholes

A board extender from the GY. When sent from hand or Deck to GY, it revives another Tindangle face-down.

Payoffs & Protection

Tindangle Delaunay

The heart of the deck. Its GY effect banishes itself to Special Summon 3 different "Tindangle" monsters from the GY face-down.

Tindangle Acute Cerberus

The archetypal boss monster. A LINK-3 that gains 3000 ATK, making it the primary in-archetype win condition.

Nagel's Protection

Protects your monsters from destruction and doubles battle damage, enabling powerful OTK strategies.

The Jhrelth Ignition

The deck's most common and powerful opening converts a single monster into a foundational board state, perfectly setting up the Graveyard for the deck's explosive follow-up plays.

Tindangle Acute Cerberus
  • Win Condition: A 4500+ ATK beater unaffected by battle, capable of closing out games with ease.
  • Board Presence: Can Special Summon a "Tindangle" from hand or GY when it destroys a monster.

Subterror Behemoth Fiendess

Subterror Behemoth Fiendess
  • FLIP Enabler: Sends a FLIP monster from deck to GY to extend combos.
  • Disruption: Provides a negate by flipping one of your face-down monsters. A mandatory component for modern builds.

Competitive Analysis

Strengths

  • Explosive Recursion: A resolved Delaunay is one of the most powerful recovery plays in the game.
  • High OTK Potential: Can easily generate over 8000 damage with Acute Cerberus or generic Link monsters.
  • Resilience to Wipes: Because the GY is the main resource, the deck can recover from board wipes like Raigeki.

Weaknesses

  • Graveyard Hate: Extremely vulnerable to cards like Dimension Shifter, D.D. Crow, and Bystials.
  • Starter Negation: A single hand trap like Ash Blossom on Jhrelth can often end the turn.
  • Inherent Slowness: The FLIP mechanic is fundamentally slower than most modern strategies, requiring a turn of setup.

Key External Engines

  • Subterror: Subterror Behemoth Fiendess is considered mandatory for enabling combos.
  • Shaddoll: Offers a potent going-second strategy with Shaddoll Fusion and disruption with El Shaddoll Winda.
  • Fiendsmith: A modern engine providing negation and targeted GY setup that synergizes with DARK Fiends.

Current TCG Banlist Status

UNRESTRICTED STATUS: The Tindangle archetype remains fully available in the current TCG format, allowing it to operate at its full potential.

Meta Implications: With no restrictions, Tindangle can utilize its complete arsenal of starters, extenders, and payoffs. This allows for maximum consistency in executing its GY-focused, "Turn 3 Lethal" strategy and full access to powerful external engines like Subterror and Shaddoll.
