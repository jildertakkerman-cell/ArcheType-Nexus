Mayakashi

The Spectral Ascent: A Strategic Analysis

Executive Summary

The Mayakashi archetype is a multi-attribute collection of Zombie monsters centered around a unique, recursive Synchro and Link Summoning strategy. Its core gameplay revolves around rapidly filling the Graveyard (GY) with Extra Deck monsters to enable a resilient, long-term grind game. Understanding the specific role of each of these spectral entities is paramount to unlocking the archetype's intricate combo potential.

The Mayakashi Engine

Starters: Initiating the Climb

Hajun, the Winged Mayakashi: The premier one-card starter, Special Summoning any Mayakashi from the Deck.

Mayakashi Return: A versatile searcher that can add any Mayakashi card to hand or send it to the GY.

Engine Core: Heart of the Combo

Dakki, the Graceful Mayakashi: The pivotal Tuner that revives itself from the GY whenever a Mayakashi is summoned from the Extra Deck. This is not a once-per-turn effect.

Yuki-Musume, the Ice Mayakashi: A powerful extender that can summon itself from the hand or GY, sending a Zombie from Deck to GY as cost.

Extenders & Support

Ghost Meets Girl: A devastating floodgate trap that stops all Special Summons for a turn.

Mayakashi Winter: Provides recursion by banishing itself from the GY to revive a Mayakashi.

Mastering the Mayakashi Ladder

The Mayakashi strategy is a two-part process. On your turn, you perform the "Ascent" to climb into powerful monsters and fill your Graveyard. On your opponent's turn, you unleash the "Descent," a chain reaction of revivals that disrupts their plays.

1

The Synchro Ascent

Start with your Level 1/3 monster and the Level 2 Tuner Dakki. Each time you Synchro Summon, Dakki revives from the GY, allowing you to immediately climb to the next level.

Goal: Fill your GY with all five Synchro monsters.

The Revival Descent

On your opponent's turn, when your high-level Synchro is destroyed, you revive the next one down from your GY, triggering a powerful disruptive effect.

LV11 Gashadokuro: Becomes unaffected by card effects.

LV9 Yoko: Destroys 1 monster.

LV7 Tengu: Destroys 1 Spell/Trap.

LV5 Tsuchigumo: Mills 3 cards from each Deck.

LV3 Oboro-Guruma: Grants battle protection for the turn.

Goal: Punish your opponent's actions with a chain of effects.

Ideal End Board & Strategy

A strong Mayakashi end board isn't about multiple omni-negates, but about creating layered, recursive threats that disrupt the opponent. The ideal setup combines on-field presence with a fully stocked Graveyard.

Yuki-Onna, the Icicle Mayakashi

1. High-Value Link Monster

End on a Link-3 or Link-4 like Yuki-Onna, the Icicle Mayakashi. This provides crucial monster negation and a GY effect to revive a Zombie Synchro on the opponent's turn, restarting your chain of disruption.

Yoko, the Graceful Mayakashi

2. Revived Synchro Monster

Use an extender like Mezuki to bring back a high-impact Synchro like Yoko, the Graceful Mayakashi. This applies immediate pressure with its non-targeting monster destruction effect.

Ghost Meets Girl

3. Set Spell/Trap Support

Search and Set a powerful trap. Ghost Meets Girl - A Masterful Mayakashi Shiranui Saga is the prime choice, acting as a searchable floodgate that can end the opponent's turn on its own.

4. Fully Loaded Graveyard

This is the most crucial component. Having the entire Synchro ladder (Levels 3, 5, 7, 9, 11) in the GY is non-negotiable, as it is the foundation of your entire grind game and turn-two disruption.

Competitive Analysis & Banlist Status

Strengths

  • Resilience: The deck thrives on destruction, turning your opponent's removal into your advantage.
  • Zombie Synergy: Excellent synergy with generic Zombie support like Zombie World and Doomking Balerdroch.
  • Grind Game: Unmatched in a long game due to its constant recursion from the Graveyard.

Weaknesses

  • Graveyard Hate: Highly vulnerable to cards like D.D. Crow or Called by the Grave on Dakki.
  • Non-Destruction Removal: Banish or bounce effects bypass the "Revival Descent" mechanic entirely.
  • Linear Combo: The core combo is predictable and has clear choke points.
