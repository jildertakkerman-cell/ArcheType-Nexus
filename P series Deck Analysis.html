:P SERIES

A Metagame Analysis of a Link Engine

Executive Summary

The informal ":P" series, consisting of I:P Masquerena, S:P Little Knight, and EM:P Meowmine, represents a cornerstone of modern deck building. This engine, linked by a shared naming convention and deep synergistic design, provides generic, powerful, and recursive interruptions. Its core function is to convert a minimal board presence into multiple layers of reactive disruption during the opponent's turn, making it one of the most efficient and widespread Extra Deck packages in the competitive Yu-Gi-Oh! TCG.

Deconstructing the Trio

I:P Masquerena

I:P Masquerena

The foundational piece. Her Quick Effect allows for a Link Summon during the opponent's turn, turning any Link monster with an on-summon effect into a powerful interruption.

S:P Little Knight

S:P Little Knight

The apex of utility and Masquerena's prime target. Provides two powerful banishing effects: one for removal on summon, and a second to interrupt or dodge opposing effects.

EM:P Meowmine

EM:P Meowmine

The recursive extender. It acts as a free Special Summon to enable combos and provides a follow-up bounce effect that triggers when I:P or S:P are used.

The Core Sequence: A Formidable End Board

The engine's primary strength is its ability to establish a simple yet powerful end board. By ending their turn with I:P Masquerena and one other monster, a player threatens to unleash a devastating series of interruptions. This single play creates a chain of effects that can dismantle an opponent's turn, showcasing the formidable combined potential of the entire :P series.

1Opponent's Turn Activation

In response to an opponent's play, activate I:P Masquerena's Quick Effect to Link Summon.

2Summon S:P Little Knight

Using I:P and another monster, summon S:P Little Knight. I:P's effect grants it protection from destruction by card effects.

3Chain of Interruptions

S:P's on-summon effect banishes a card. If EM:P Meowmine is in the GY/hand, its effect also triggers to bounce a card. S:P then remains on field, threatening a second banish effect.

S:P Little Knight

Competitive Analysis

Strengths

  • Generic & Flexible: Fits into any deck that can make Link-2s.
  • Resource Efficient: Generates multiple interruptions from a small investment.
  • Built-in Protection: I:P grants destruction immunity, while S:P can dodge targeted effects.

Weaknesses

  • Effect Negation: Vulnerable to hand traps like Effect Veiler or Infinite Impermanence on I:P.
  • Timing Exploits: I:P can only be activated in the Main Phase, leaving it vulnerable to plays in other phases.
  • Battle Removal: S:P Little Knight's low ATK makes it susceptible to being destroyed by battle.

Top Tier Integration

  • Snake-Eye/Fire King: Effortlessly makes the engine as part of its main combo.
  • Live Twin/Spright: The ideal home for the full 3-card engine due to its focus on Link-2 monsters.
  • Orcust: Provides flexible, non-DARK-locking interaction.

Current TCG Banlist Status

The core ":P" engine remains largely untouched, but some of its most powerful historic partners have been forbidden, altering its application in the metagame.

Unlimited

  • I:P Masquerena
  • S:P Little Knight
  • EM:P Meowmine

Limited

  • Knightmare Unicorn (Often Replaced by S:P)

Forbidden

  • Apollousa, Bow of the Goddess
  • Spright Elf

Meta Implications: While the core I:P -> S:P play is at full strength, the banning of generic bosses like Apollousa and extenders like Spright Elf has narrowed the engine's highest-power end boards, making the choice of what to Link into more strategic. S:P Little Knight remains the default, most powerful option.
