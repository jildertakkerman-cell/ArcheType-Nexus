SYNCHRON

Analysis of a High-Velocity Synchro Engine

Executive Summary

The "Synchron" archetype is a renowned Synchro-based strategy, famous for its high-velocity, "Synchro climbing" combo lines. Evolving from a fragile Normal Summon-reliant build, the modern engine is a robust, multi-faceted system that leverages the hand, Graveyard, and Deck to establish overwhelming endboards. Its identity is defined by the lynchpin card Junk Speeder, which explodes onto the field to generate a critical mass of Tuners.

Recent "Power of Fellows" support has fundamentally reshaped the deck, solving its decades-old weaknesses. New cards like "Synchro Fellowship" fix "bricky" hands and provide recovery, while the "Scrap" package (Scrap Synchron & Scrap Warrior) provides a new combo line that makes "Junk Speeder" completely immune to "Ash Blossom" and other negation.

The Synchron Arsenal

Core Monsters: Starters & Extenders

Junk Synchron

Junk Synchron

The classic Normal Summon starter. Revives a Level 2 or lower monster to immediately make "Junk Speeder".

Scrap Synchron

A new utility Tuner. Can be used as Synchro Material from the hand and banishes from GY to protect "Warrior" Synchros from destruction.

Fullspeed Warrior

A new non-Tuner searcher. On summon, adds "Junk Synchron" or a Spell/Trap mentioning "Junk Warrior". A prime discard target for "Junk Converter".

Stardust Synchron

A modern starter and extender. Summons itself from hand/GY and searches "Stardust" Spells/Traps.

Jet & Assault Synchron

Key extenders that Special Summon themselves from the hand (Assault) or GY (Jet), enabling combos before the Normal Summon.

Key Spells: Consistency & Recovery

Synchro Fellowship

Synchro Fellowship

The new heart of the deck. Adds "Junk Synchron" & another piece, then discards 1 to fix "bricky" hands. Its GY effect (banish, target Synchro, reduce Level, gain Normal Summon) is the deck's new resource-less "Plan B" if "Junk Speeder" is negated.

Junk Converter

A critical consistency tool. Discard itself and a Tuner to search any "Synchron" monster, loading the GY in the process. Its GY effect is key for chain-blocking.

Tuning

The signature search Spell. Adds any "Synchron" Tuner and mills one card, which can be a huge benefit for GY setup.

Synchro Overtake

Value has skyrocketed. It's no longer just a "Junk Synchron" searcher; it's the primary enabler for the "Ash-Proof" combo by summoning Scrap Synchron from the Deck (by revealing "Scrap Warrior").

Core Combo Walkthrough

The new support provides two distinct and powerful lines: a hyper-consistent 1.5-card starter, and a more complex but fully-protected "Ash-Proof" combo.

Line 1: The 1.5-Card "Fellowship" Starter

Start Hand: "Synchro Fellowship" + 1 discard fodder (e.g., "Junk Converter" or a "garnet" like "Jet Synchron").

Synchro Fellowship
1

Search & Setup

Activate Synchro Fellowship. Add Junk Synchron and Junk Converter from Deck to hand. Then, discard the Junk Converter to set up the GY. This also readies "Fellowship's" GY effect for a "Plan B".

Junk Speeder
2

Summon & Chain Block

Normal Summon Junk Synchron (revives "Converter"). Synchro into Junk Speeder. Build Chain: CL1: Speeder (on-summon), CL2: Converter (GY effect). This "Chain Block" protects Speeder's effect from "Ash Blossom".

T.G. Hyper Librarian
3

The Climb

"Junk Speeder" resolves, summoning a board full of Tuners. From here, you proceed with the standard Synchro climb into T.G. Hyper Librarian, Junk Warrior, and Crystal Wing to build your endboard.

Line 2: The Optimal "Ash-Proof" Gambit

Start Hand: "Synchro Overtake" + "Junk Synchron" + Level 2 Extender (e.g., "Assault Synchron").

Synchro Overtake
1

Summon Scrap Tuner

Activate Synchro Overtake, revealing Scrap Warrior from your Extra Deck. Special Summon Scrap Synchron (Lvl 1 Tuner) from your Deck.

Scrap Warrior
2

Establish Protection

Special Summon your Lvl 2 Extender (e.g., Assault Synchron). Synchro Summon Scrap Warrior (Lvl 3) using Scrap Synchron + Assault Synchron.
CRITICAL: "Scrap Warrior's" continuous effect is now active, preventing your "Warrior" Synchros' effects from being negated.

Junk Speeder
3

The "Ash-Proof" Speeder

"Scrap Warrior's" on-summon effect triggers: send Junk Converter from Deck to GY.
Normal Summon Junk Synchron, reviving Junk Converter.
Synchro Summon Junk Speeder.
Result: "Junk Speeder" is a "Warrior" Synchro, so "Scrap Warrior" makes its effect un-negatable. You also chain-block with "Converter" (CL1: Speeder, CL2: Converter) for full protection. It cannot be stopped.

Typical Endboard: The "Warrior" Package

Junk Warrior

Junk Warrior
  • Thematic Boss Monster: A "Warrior" Synchro that gains the ATK of all Level 2 or lower monsters on your field.
  • "Warrior" Synergy: As a "Warrior" Synchro, its activated ATK-gain effect is protected by Scrap Warrior's continuous effect (it cannot be negated).
  • Destruction Protection: Can be protected from destruction (once) by Scrap Synchron's GY effect.

Crystal Wing Synchro Dragon

Crystal Wing Synchro Dragon
  • Monster Negate: A dedicated Quick Effect negate for any *other* monster's effect.
  • ATK Gain: Destroys the negated monster and gains its ATK.
  • The "Dragon" Problem: As a "Dragon," this card is NOT protected by the new "Scrap" support, creating a new deck-building tension.

New Endboard Tension: "Warrior" vs. "Dragon"

The new support creates a strategic choice. Scrap Synchron and Scrap Warrior only protect "Warrior" Synchros. This makes "Junk Warrior" and other "Warrior" bosses safer than ever. However, powerful "Dragon" bosses like "Crystal Wing" and "Cosmic Blazar Dragon" are left unprotected, forcing the player to choose between raw power and built-in resilience.

The "Junk Warrior Extreme" Trap

Do not play "Junk Warrior Extreme" in this deck. Its effect (reviving Lvl 2s) imposes a lock: "...you can only Special Summon one more time for the rest of this turn." This KILLS YOUR COMBO. It is a boss monster for a different, casual OTK build and actively conflicts with the "Synchro climbing" strategy.

Competitive Analysis (Post-Support)

Strengths

  • Explosive Ceiling: Still capable of ending on 4-5 negates.
  • Extreme Consistency: "Synchro Fellowship" is a +1 starter that stacks with "Tuning" and "Converter".
  • "Ash-Proof" Combo: The "Scrap Warrior" line proactively solves the deck's primary choke point.
  • "Garnet" Fixing: "Synchro Fellowship" turns "bricky" hands into perfect setup by providing a targeted discard.
  • Resource-less "Plan B": "Fellowship's" GY effect is a free, powerful pivot if "Speeder" is stopped.

Weaknesses

  • Vulnerable to "Nibiru" (Worse): This is the big one. The new "Scrap Warrior" combo worsens the summon count, requiring 3-4 summons before "Speeder" even hits the field, making "Nibiru" even more devastating.
  • Vulnerable to "DRNM": "Dark Ruler No More" is still a massive problem.
    (Mitigation): "Scrap Synchron's" GY effect can protect Junk Warrior from being destroyed after "DRNM" resolves, providing follow-up.
  • "Warrior" vs "Dragon" Tension: Your best protection only works on "Warrior" bosses, forcing a difficult choice in Extra Deck building.

External Engines & Tech

  • Adventurer Engine: Used to establish Wandering Gryphon Rider *before* the combo to protect "Junk Speeder" (a different anti-negation route).
  • Diabellstar Engine: A versatile engine that provides a body and a way to send Jet Synchron from hand to GY to summon one from Deck.
  • Crossout Designator: Used to protect the combo from key hand traps like "Ash Blossom" or (especially) "Nibiru".

Current TCG Banlist Status

FULL POWER STATUS: Unlike many other combo decks, the core "Synchron" archetype is almost entirely unrestricted.

Restricted Cards

  • Reinforcement of the Army (1 copy) - Limits access to "Junk Synchron" & "Junk Converter".
  • Forbidden Enablers: Key generic combo enablers like Baronne de Fleur, Chaos Ruler, the Chaotic Magical Dragon and Crystron Halqifibrax are **Forbidden**, shaping modern deck building.

Unrestricted Cards

  • Synchro Fellowship (3 copies) - The new core is at full power.
  • Junk Speeder (3 copies) - The core lynchpin is at full power.
  • All Main Deck "Synchron" Tuners (3 copies each)
  • Junk Converter, Tuning, Synchro Overtake (3 copies each)

Meta Implications: With its entire core engine at 3 and new support solving its main weaknesses, "Synchron" is one of the most consistent and explosive combo decks available. Its main limitations come from the banning of generic enablers ("Chaos Ruler", "Baronne"), not its own archetype, forcing it to rely on its (now highly resilient) in-archetype lines.
