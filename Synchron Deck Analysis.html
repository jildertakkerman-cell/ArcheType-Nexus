SYNCHRON

Mastering the Accelerator Engine

Executive Summary

The Synchron archetype is a high-performance engine composed of specialized parts, each with a precise function. It thrives on explosive, sequential Synchro Summoning to build formidable end boards. The deck's modern strategy revolves around the successful resolution of a single, overwhelmingly powerful monster: Junk Speeder. The entire early game is a calculated effort to summon and resolve this one card, transitioning from methodical setup to an overwhelming combo execution that defines the deck's competitive potential.

The Core Engine

The Igniters

Starters and consistency tools that initiate the combo chain.

Junk Synchron: The quintessential starter. Its Normal Summon revives a Level 2 or lower monster, instantly setting up a Level 5 Synchro.

Junk Converter: A pivotal consistency tool. Discard itself and a Tuner to search for any "Synchron" monster.

Tuning: The dedicated searcher spell, adding any "Synchron" monster and milling one card.

The Fuel

Combo extenders that provide additional materials for climbing into higher-Level Synchros.

Jet Synchron: A premier extender that can Special Summon itself from the Graveyard. (Currently Limited)

Assault Synchron: Special Summons itself from hand at the cost of 700 LP, providing a free body but locking you into Synchro Summons.

Doppelwarrior: Summons itself from hand when a monster is revived, and generates tokens when used as Synchro material.

The Second Hand

The Graveyard is a secondary resource pool, turning costs into strategic setup plays.

Cards like Jet Synchron and Plaguespreader Zombie can revive themselves, extending combos. The secondary effect of Junk Converter revives a Tuner used for a Synchro Summon, directly continuing the chain.

The Heart of the Engine: Junk Speeder

Junk Speeder

The entire Synchron strategy is built to summon and resolve Junk Speeder. On Synchro Summon, it Special Summons a swarm of "Synchron" Tuners with different Levels from the Deck. This single resolution provides an explosive generation of card advantage and board presence, converting an investment of two monsters into a field of up to five additional monsters, ready for the Synchro Climb.

The Synchro Lock

To balance its immense power, Junk Speeder imposes a crucial restriction: you cannot Special Summon from the Extra Deck, except for Synchro Monsters, for the rest of the turn. This forces a commitment to the Synchro mechanic.

The Key Summon

Normal Summon Junk Synchron. Activate its effect, targeting Junk Converter in your GY to Special Summon it. Immediately Synchro Summon Junk Speeder.

Chain Block & Resolution

Upon summon, two effects trigger. Chain Link 1 is Junk Speeder, Chain Link 2 is Junk Converter. This "chain blocking" protects Speeder's effect from negation. The chain resolves, reviving a Tuner and then swarming the field with more Tuners from the Deck.

The Final Board: Fortress of Disruption

The culmination of a successful Synchron combo is an oppressive endboard, a carefully constructed fortress of synergistic boss monsters designed to dismantle the opponent's turn. The strength of this board lies not merely in the number of interruptions but in their variety.

TCG Banlist Impact

The Synchron archetype has been impacted by the TCG banlist, primarily through hits to its most powerful generic tools and extenders.

Limited Cards (1 per Deck)

  • Jet Synchron: A key self-reviving extender.

Forbidden Cards (0 per Deck)

  • Borreload Savage Dragon: A major loss for the deck's end board negation capabilities.
  • Baronne de Fleur: Removes one of the game's most versatile omni-negates and board presence tools.

Meta Implications: While the core Synchron engine remains largely intact, the limitation of key extenders like Jet Synchron and the bans on staples like Borreload Savage Dragon and Baronne de Fleur requires players to adapt their end boards and combo routes, relying more on other powerful Synchros like Bystial Dis Pater and Crystal Wing Synchro Dragon.
