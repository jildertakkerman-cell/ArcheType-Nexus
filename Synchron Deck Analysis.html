SYNCHRON

Analysis of a High-Velocity Synchro Engine

Executive Summary

The "Synchron" archetype is a renowned Synchro-based strategy, famous for its high-velocity, "Synchro climbing" combo lines. Evolving from a fragile Normal Summon-reliant build, the modern engine is a robust, multi-faceted system that leverages the hand, Graveyard, and Deck to establish overwhelming endboards. Its identity is defined by the lynchpin card Junk Speeder, which explodes onto the field to generate a critical mass of Tuners.

Recent support has fundamentally reshaped the deck, solving its decades-old weaknesses. New cards like "Synchro Fellowship" and "Scrap Synchron" have transformed it from a "glass cannon" into a resilient and highly consistent combo engine with built-in protection and recovery.

The Synchron Arsenal

Core Monsters: Starters & Extenders

Junk Synchron

Junk Synchron

The classic Normal Summon starter. Revives a Level 2 or lower monster to immediately make "Junk Speeder".

Scrap Synchron

A new utility Tuner. Can be used from the *hand* for a Synchro Summon and banishes from GY to protect "Warrior" Synchros.

Stardust Synchron

A modern starter and extender. Summons itself from hand/GY and searches "Stardust" Spells/Traps.

Jet & Assault Synchron

Key extenders that Special Summon themselves from the hand (Assault) or GY (Jet), enabling combos *before* the Normal Summon.

Key Spells: Consistency & Recovery

Synchro Fellowship

Synchro Fellowship

The new heart of the deck. A +1 searcher that adds "Junk Synchron" & another piece, then discards 1 to fix "bricky" hands. Its GY effect is a "Plan B" if your combo is stopped.

Junk Converter

A critical consistency tool. Discard itself and a Tuner to search any "Synchron" monster, loading the GY in the process.

Tuning

The signature search Spell. Adds any "Synchron" Tuner and mills one card, which can be a huge benefit for GY setup.

Synchro Overtake

A powerful searcher that reveals a Synchro (e.g., "Junk Warrior") to summon its "Synchron" material (e.g., "Junk Synchron") from the Deck.

Core Combo Walkthrough

The entire deck is built to resolve "Junk Speeder". The baseline combo is simple, but new support has created a much more powerful and resilient line.

Modern Combo: The "Fellowship" Protected Line

Start Hand: "Synchro Fellowship" + "Junk Converter"

Synchro Fellowship
1

Advantage & Setup

Activate Synchro Fellowship. Add Junk Synchron and Scrap Synchron from Deck to hand. Then, discard Junk Converter to set up the GY.

Junk Speeder
2

Summon & Chain Block

Normal Summon Junk Synchron (revives "Converter"). Synchro into Junk Speeder. Build Chain: CL1: Speeder, CL2: Converter (reviving "Scrap Synchron"). This "Chain Block" protects Speeder's effect from "Ash Blossom".

Scrap Warrior
3

Establish Protection

Speeder summons its Tuners. Now, use the revived Scrap Synchron (Lvl 1) + a Lvl 2 Tuner from Deck to Synchro Summon Scrap Warrior (Lvl 3). Its continuous effect now prevents your "Warrior" Synchros' activated effects from being negated!

T.G. Hyper Librarian
4

The Protected Climb

With Scrap Warrior protecting your field, you can now safely climb into T.G. Hyper Librarian, Junk Warrior, and other "Warrior" bosses, drawing cards and building your board without fear of "Effect Veiler" or "Impermanence".

Typical Endboard: The "Warrior" Package

Junk Warrior

Junk Warrior
  • Thematic Boss Monster: A "Warrior" Synchro that gains the ATK of all Level 2 or lower monsters on your field.
  • Now Protected: Its on-summon ATK gain effect is protected by Scrap Warrior's aura.
  • Also Protected: Can be protected from destruction (once) by Scrap Synchron's GY effect.

Crystal Wing Synchro Dragon

Crystal Wing Synchro Dragon
  • Monster Negate: A dedicated Quick Effect negate for any *other* monster's effect.
  • ATK Gain: Destroys the negated monster and gains its ATK.
  • The "Dragon" Problem: As a "Dragon," this card is NOT protected by the new "Scrap" support, creating a new deck-building tension.

New Endboard Tension: "Warrior" vs. "Dragon"

The new support creates a strategic choice. Scrap Synchron and Scrap Warrior *only* protect "Warrior" Synchros. This makes "Baronne de Fleur" and "Junk Warrior" safer than ever. However, powerful "Dragon" bosses like "Crystal Wing" and "Cosmic Blazar Dragon" are left unprotected, forcing the player to choose between raw power and built-in resilience.

Competitive Analysis (Post-Support)

Strengths

  • Explosive Ceiling: Still capable of ending on 4-5 negates.
  • Extreme Consistency: "Synchro Fellowship" is a +1 starter that stacks with "Tuning" and "Converter".
  • In-Engine Protection: "Scrap Warrior" and "Scrap Synchron" provide built-in negation and destruction protection.
  • "Garnet" Fixing: "Fellowship" turns "bricky" hands into perfect setup by providing a targeted discard.

Weaknesses

  • "Speeder" Choke Point (Mitigated): Resolving "Junk Speeder" is still key, but Fellowship's GY effect provides a powerful "Plan B" recovery if it's negated.
  • Vulnerable to "Nibiru" (Mitigated): Still a threat, but new support makes it easier to summon Herald of the Arc Light *before* the 5th summon.
  • Vulnerable to "DRNM": "Dark Ruler No More" is still a massive problem, as "Scrap Warrior" only stops *activated effects*, not un-respondable cards.
  • "Warrior" vs "Dragon" Tension: Your best protection only works on "Warrior" bosses, forcing a difficult choice in Extra Deck building.

External Engines & Tech

  • Adventurer Engine: Used to establish Wandering Gryphon Rider *before* the combo to protect "Junk Speeder".
  • Diabellstar Engine: A versatile engine that provides a body and a way to send Jet Synchron from hand to GY to summon one from Deck.
  • Crossout Designator: Used to protect the combo from key hand traps like "Ash Blossom" or "Nibiru".

Current TCG Banlist Status

FULL POWER STATUS: Unlike many other combo decks, the core "Synchron" archetype is almost entirely unrestricted.

Restricted Cards

  • Reinforcement of the Army (1 copy) - Limits access to "Junk Synchron" & "Junk Converter".
  • Forbidden Generics: Key generic bosses like Baronne de Fleur, Chaos Ruler, and Crystron Halqifibrax are **Forbidden**, shaping modern end boards.

Unrestricted Cards

  • Synchro Fellowship (3 copies) - The new core is at full power.
  • Junk Speeder (3 copies) - The core lynchpin is at full power.
  • All Main Deck "Synchron" Tuners (3 copies each)
  • Junk Converter, Tuning, Synchro Overtake (3 copies each)

Meta Implications: With its entire core engine at 3 and new support solving its main weaknesses, "Synchron" is one of the most consistent and explosive combo decks available. Its main limitations come from the banning of generic enablers ("Chaos Ruler"), not its own archetype, forcing it to rely on its (now highly resilient) in-archetype lines.
