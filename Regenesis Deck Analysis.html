Regenesis

A Deep Dive into the 2500 ATK/DEF Engine

Executive Summary

The Regenesis archetype is a control-oriented, resource-grinding engine that excels in protracted duels. Its core mechanic is a strict dependency on monsters with 2500 original ATK or DEF, forcing it to integrate with external engines. The archetype's primary identity is defined by its ability to maintain a self-sustaining resource loop that fuels a long-term grind game, largely independent of the Extra Deck.

Core Mechanics & Philosophy

The "2500" Gimmick

The archetype's monsters require a monster with 2500 original ATK or DEF on the field to be Special Summoned, creating a deliberate dependency on external "host" archetypes like Kashtira, Bystial, or Dogmatika to start its plays.

The Grind Game

Core monsters return to the hand from the GY during the opponent's End Phase. This creates a relentless resource loop, allowing the deck to out-value opponents in a war of attrition rather than aiming for a swift OTK.

Main Deck Supremacy

Key cards like Regenesis Code lock the player out of the Extra Deck. This guides the strategy towards Main Deck-focused engines and makes it inherently resilient to common Extra Deck counters like Kaijus or Super Polymerization.

The Regenesis Cycle: Key Cards

Regenesis Archfiend

The primary starter. Special Summons itself from hand and searches any "Regenesis" card, acting as the main consistency tool.

Regenesis Sage

The archetype's omni-negate. A Quick Effect that sends another "Regenesis" card to the GY to negate and destroy an opponent's card or effect.

Regenesis Dragon

Dedicated backrow removal. Tributes itself as a Quick Effect to target and banish one Spell/Trap the opponent controls.

Regenesis Warrior

Monster removal. Tributes itself as a Quick Effect to target an opponent's monster and return it to the hand, providing non-destruction interaction.

Regenesis Lord

The main boss monster. Summons itself by banishing a 2500 ATK/DEF monster and Sets a "Regenesis" Spell/Trap directly from the Deck.

Regenesis Birth

A versatile Continuous Trap that can send a "Regenesis" monster from Deck to GY to disrupt, or send itself to mass-summon "Regenesis" monsters from hand, GY, or banishment.

Core Combo: Archfiend + Enabler

This two-card combination represents the deck's most efficient opening play, establishing its core resource loop and interaction points.

Interaction Paths:

  • Activate Regenesis Birth to flip an opponent's monster face-down and send Regenesis Sage to the GY.
  • Send Regenesis Birth to the GY to Special Summon the banished Archfiend and the GY Sage, establishing an on-field omni-negate.
  • Monsters sent to GY during the opponent's turn will return to your hand in their End Phase.

Competitive Analysis

Strengths

  • Endless Resources: The recursion loop makes it incredibly difficult to out-grind in a long duel.
  • Engine Versatility: Can hybridize with any strong archetype that provides a 2500 ATK/DEF body, making it future-proof.
  • Resilience: Independence from the Extra Deck makes it immune to many common board-breaking strategies.

Weaknesses

  • Choke Points: Relies heavily on resolving the initial 2500 ATK/DEF monster and the Archfiend search.
  • Graveyard Hate: Cards like Dimension Shifter or Bystial monsters can permanently break the resource loop.
  • Floodgates: Highly vulnerable to cards like Skill Drain and Anti-Spell Fragrance.

Synergistic Engines

  • Kashtira: Provides easy Level 7 bodies for Rank 7 Xyz plays and zone-locking.
  • Bystial: Offers GY disruption and powerful starters like The Bystial Lubellion.
  • Dogmatika: Highly consistent engine that generates advantage without needing the Normal Summon.

TCG Banlist Impact

FULL POWER: As a newer archetype, Regenesis currently has no cards on the Forbidden & Limited list. However, its performance is influenced by the status of its partner engines.

Unrestricted Cards

  • All Regenesis Main Deck monsters (3 copies each)
  • All Regenesis Spell/Trap cards (3 copies each)

External Engine Status

  • Limited: Key generic staples that may be used, such as Terraforming.
  • Varies by partner engine (e.g., status of Kashtira or Bystial cards).
