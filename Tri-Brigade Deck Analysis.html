Tri-Brigade

Analysis of the Revolt

Executive Summary

The Tri-Brigade archetype is a versatile and highly efficient engine centered around Beast, Beast-Warrior, and Winged Beast monsters. Their unique summoning mechanic, which banishes monsters from the Graveyard to Link Summon, allows for explosive plays and rapid board presence. The deck's strategy revolves around loading the Graveyard with "Tri-Type" monsters to fuel their powerful summons, culminating in a disruptive end board that can control the game. This adaptability has made Tri-Brigade a consistent threat in various competitive formats, capable of functioning as a pure strategy or a potent engine in numerous hybrid decks.

The Core Engine: Arsenal of the Revolt

Main Deck Warriors

Tri-Brigade Fraktall

The premier starter. Sends itself to the GY to load another Tri-Type monster, initiating the core combo.

Tri-Brigade Kitt

A key combo piece. When sent to the GY, she sends another Tri-Brigade card from the Deck to the GY.

Tri-Brigade Nervall

The primary searcher. When sent to the GY, he adds a Tri-Brigade monster from Deck to hand.

Tri-Brigade Kerass

An extender that can be Special Summoned from the hand, enabling further Link plays.

Extra Deck Commanders

Tri-Brigade Ferrijit the Barren Blossom

A Link-2 extender that Special Summons a monster from hand.

Tri-Brigade Bearbrumm the Rampant Rampager

The Link-2 that searches for the powerful Tri-Brigade Revolt trap card.

Tri-Brigade Shuraig the Ominous Omen

The main boss monster. A Link-4 that provides non-targeting banishing removal.

Foundational One-Card Combo

The deck's consistency shines through its one-card starter combos. With just a single Tri-Brigade Fraktall, a player can establish a formidable board presence and set up powerful disruptions for the opponent's turn. This sequence demonstrates the efficient resource conversion that makes the Tri-Brigade forces so threatening.

Tri-Brigade Fraktall
1

Load the Graveyard

Activate Fraktall's effect from hand, sending it to the GY to send Kitt from Deck to GY. Kitt's effect then sends Nervall. Nervall's effect adds Kerass to hand.

Tri-Brigade Bearbrumm
2

Summon and Search

Normal Summon Kerass. Use its effect, banishing 2 from GY to summon Bearbrumm. Link Kerass and Bearbrumm for Ferrijit. Chain block with Ferrijit as Chain Link 2 to protect Bearbrumm's search for Revolt.

Competitive Analysis

Strengths

  • High Consistency: Numerous one-card starters make the deck reliable.
  • Resource Loop: The GY and banish zone are treated as a revolving door of resources.
  • Versatile Engine: Can be splashed into many Beast, Beast-Warrior, and Winged Beast strategies.

Weaknesses

  • Graveyard Hate: Highly vulnerable to cards like Dimension Shifter and D.D. Crow.
  • Choke Points: The initial Fraktall or Kitt effect is a major choke point for hand traps like Ash Blossom.
  • Board Breakers: Can struggle against cards like Dark Ruler No More and Evenly Matched.

Popular Hybrids

  • Lyrilusc ("Bird Up"): An explosive variant creating oppressive boards with multiple negates.
  • Branded: Combines Tri-Brigade consistency with the raw power of Mirrorjade and Branded Fusion.
  • Zoodiac: A classic pairing that adds easy access to Zeus and extra disruption.

TCG Banlist Impact

FULLY UNRESTRICTED: The core Tri-Brigade archetype has no cards on the current TCG Forbidden & Limited list.

Meta Implications: With its entire engine at full power, Tri-Brigade remains a highly viable and flexible strategy. While some of its most powerful generic tools and hybrid partners have been hit by the banlist over time, the core consistency and power of the Tri-Brigade monsters themselves remain untouched, allowing the deck to adapt and thrive in various competitive environments.
