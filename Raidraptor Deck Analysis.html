Raidraptor

The Revolution's Ascent

Executive Summary

The Raidraptor archetype, a squadron of DARK Winged-Beast monsters, defines an explosive, swarming playstyle. Its core strategy is a rapid escalation of threats, using a multitude of Main Deck monsters to quickly assemble materials for Xyz Summons. This foundation is then leveraged by its signature mechanic: the use of "Rank-Up-Magic" (RUM) Spell Cards to evolve its Xyz Monsters into progressively more powerful, higher-Rank forms, culminating in game-ending boss monsters.

Core Philosophy: Swarm, Rank-Up, Dominate

Swarm & Pivot

The primary game plan involves using Level 4 extenders to flood the field, enabling the summon of Raidraptor - Force Strix. This key Xyz monster searches for combo pieces, which are then used to Link Summon Raidraptor - Wise Strix, the deck's central enabler.

Rank-Up & Dominate

Wise Strix sets a "Rank-Up-Magic" Spell from the deck, initiating the archetype's signature mechanic. This allows for the evolution of smaller Xyz monsters into imposing threats, most notably the Rank 13 behemoth, Raidraptor - Rising Rebellion Falcon.

Resulting Interruptions & Follow-up

  • Full Board Wipe: Activate the set Rise Rank-Up-Magic Raidraptor's Force on the opponent's turn to summon a second Rising Rebellion Falcon, destroying all their cards.
  • Targeted Negation: Flip the set Raidraptor - Glorious Bright to negate an opponent's face-up card effect.
  • Passive Immunity: The two "Falcon" boss monsters are immune to most forms of removal, forcing specific outs.
  • Recovery & Follow-up: The remaining Force Strix and Wise Strix on field ensure resources are available to rebuild and press for victory on the following turn.

TCG Banlist Impact

UNRESTRICTED STATUS: The Raidraptor archetype currently operates at full power in the TCG format with no direct limitations.

Meta Implications: With complete access to all archetype components, including multiple copies of key starters and extenders like Tribute Lanius and Strangle Lanius, the deck maintains its explosive combo potential and multiple endboard configurations, making it a viable competitive choice. No core "Raidraptor" cards are currently on the Forbidden & Limited list.
