TENPAI DRAGON

The Art of the Battle Phase OTK

Executive Summary

The Tenpai Dragon archetype is a hyper-aggressive, "blind-second" OTK (One-Turn Kill) strategy centered around FIRE Dragon monsters. Thematically inspired by the Dragon tiles of Mahjong, the deck's defining feature is its ability to Synchro Summon during the Battle Phase, turning every attack into a potential combo extension. With remarkable consistency and a compact engine, Tenpai Dragon dedicates significant deck space to board-breaking cards, aiming to dismantle an opponent's established field and secure a swift victory on Turn 2.

The Sangenpai Dragons: Core Monsters

Tenpai Dragon Paidra

The premier starter and searcher. On summon, it adds a "Sangen" Spell/Trap from the deck, typically the crucial Field Spell, Sangen Summoning.

Tenpai Dragon Fadra

The primary recursion tool. It revives a Level 4 or lower FIRE Dragon from the Graveyard, extending plays and recycling key resources.

Tenpai Dragon Chundra

The main Tuner and extender. It can Special Summon itself from the hand and, critically, summons another FIRE Dragon directly from the deck during the Damage Step.

Blueprint for Victory

The deck's power lies in its simple, efficient combos. A single starter card can quickly escalate into a full board, leveraging the Battle Phase to Synchro climb into its devastating bosses. This core sequence showcases how to assemble the fiery draconic arsenal for an OTK.

The Inevitable Endboard

Sangenpai Transcendent Dragion
  • Battle Phase Lock: Prevents the opponent from activating any cards or effects during the Battle Phase.
  • Recursion: Can revive itself from the Graveyard once per duel after three attacks have been declared.

Trident Dragion

Trident Dragion

Competitive Analysis

Strengths

  • Unmatched Consistency: High number of one-card starters makes the OTK highly probable.
  • Explosive Power: Capable of generating astronomical damage in a single turn.
  • Built-in Protection: Key combo pieces activate in the Damage Step, dodging common handtraps like Ash Blossom.

Weaknesses

  • Specific Choke Points: Highly vulnerable to handtraps like Droll & Lock Bird or negation on the initial starter monster.
  • Reliance on Battle Phase: Cards that skip or prevent the Battle Phase (e.g., Threatening Roar) completely stop the deck.
  • Fragile to Board Wipes: Vulnerable to non-targeting board clears like Dark Ruler No More before the Battle Phase.

Key Tech Choices

  • Pot of Prosperity: Helps find starters or board breakers. The half-damage drawback is negligible for a deck that can deal over 30,000 damage.
  • Generic Board Breakers: Raigeki, Lightning Storm, and Dark Hole are essential for clearing the opponent's board to enable the OTK.
