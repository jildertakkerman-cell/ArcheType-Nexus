Maliss

Down the Rabbit Hole of a Digital Wonderland

Archetype Overview

The Maliss archetype, first appearing in Crossover Breakers, is a DARK Cyberse Link strategy renowned for its recursive and explosive gameplay. The core mechanic involves a unique loop: banishing its own monsters to activate their effects, which then Special Summon themselves from the banished zone. This turns the banished zone into a dynamic resource pool, allowing for rapid Link climbing and the generation of immense card advantage from a single starter. The aesthetic masterfully blends themes from "Alice's Adventures in Wonderland" with the clandestine nature of computer malware, creating a surreal digital battleground. Prepare to go down the rabbit hole to understand their protocol.

The Mainframe: Core Monster Roster

Maliss Dormouse

The premier one-card starter. Its Ignition Effect banishes any "Maliss" monster from the Deck, initiating the core combo. When banished itself, it revives for 300 LP.

Maliss White Rabbit

The secondary starter. On summon, it Sets a "Maliss" Trap from the Deck, providing access to extenders or disruption. Revives from banish for 300 LP.

Maliss Chessy Cat

A key extender and consistency tool. It allows you to banish a "Maliss" card from hand to draw two cards, improving hand quality and triggering banished effects.

Maliss March Hare

Provides a free Special Summon from hand by banishing a "Maliss" card from hand or GY, enabling more explosive plays without using the Normal Summon.

Core Combo: The One-Card Dormouse

Maliss Dormouse

1 Normal Summon & Banish

Normal Summon Dormouse. Activate its effect, banishing White Rabbit from your Deck.

Maliss White Rabbit

2 Revive & Link Climb

White Rabbit's banished effect triggers. Pay 300 LP to Special Summon it. Now with two monsters, begin your Link climb into your boss monsters.

Maliss Red Ransom

3 Search and Extend

Summon Maliss Red Ransom to search for the Field Spell, Maliss in Underground, which further extends your plays by providing another banish.

Competitive Analysis

Strengths

  • High consistency with multiple one-card starters.
  • Resilient, recursive engine that is difficult to stop permanently.
  • Explosive Link climbing potential for powerful endboards.

Weaknesses

  • Extremely vulnerable to cards that prevent banishing (Artifact Lancea).
  • The Normal Summon starter is a key choke point for hand traps.
  • Susceptible to mass board removal like Nibiru, the Primal Being.

Current TCG Banlist Status

Limited (1 per Deck)

  • Maliss Dormouse

Semi-Limited (2 per Deck)

  • Maliss in Underground

Unrestricted (3 per Deck)

  • All other "Maliss" cards

Meta Implications: The limitation of Maliss Dormouse significantly impacts the deck's consistency, reducing the number of primary one-card starters. The semi-limitation of Maliss in Underground also hurts consistency. While still potent, the deck must now rely more heavily on its secondary starters and extenders to execute its core strategy.
